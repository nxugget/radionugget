## Software Part
First, download the [PI24 image](https://repo-feed.flightradar24.com/rpi_images/fr24-raspberry-pi-latest.img.zip) and use a tool like [Etcher](https://etcher.balena.io/) to write it onto your SD card.

Once your SD card is inserted and formatted, unzip the Pi24 image (so that only the file `fr24-raspberry-pi-latest.img` remains), launch Etcher, select your image, choose your SD card as the target, and hit *Flash*.

![Balena Etcher](/images/blog/2025/adsb/adsb3.webp)

When finished, don‚Äôt eject the SD card yet if you can‚Äôt connect an Ethernet cable to your Pi. You‚Äôll need to configure your Wi‚ÄëFi first. ```bash

```bash
    ssid="YOUR_SSID"  # Replace with your Wi‚ÄëFi name
    psk="YOUR_WIFI_PASSWORD"  # Replace with your Wi‚ÄëFi password

```

Now, eject the SD card and insert it into your Raspberry Pi. Once you‚Äôve hooked up your antenna to the SDR receiver (which in turn is connected to your Pi), power it on.

After booting up, create a **FlightRadar** account and visit [this link](https://www.flightradar24.com/build-your-own) which will automatically detect your Pi24 and prompt you to register it. If all goes well, you should see a page like this:

![Pi24 activation](/images/blog/2025/adsb/adsb4.webp)

Click on `Activate`, fill in your station‚Äôs latitude and longitude, verify the information, and you‚Äôll get a confirmation message along with an email containing your sharing key and radar code.
